## 4. What to Look For When Browsing

Don't just screenshot things that look pretty. Look for **solutions to your specific challenges:**

### Challenge 1: "How do I unify 6+ different domains without looking scattered?"
**Look for:** Sites that use a **single visual language** (typography, spacing, color) across wildly different content types. Study how they use **section transitions**, shared **card styles**, or a **narrative flow** rather than just nav links.

### Challenge 2: "How do I lead with identity, not a service list?"
**Look for:** Hero sections that start with a **statement about who someone is or what they believe** — not what they sell. Study sites that use **editorial headlines**, personal photography, or a **manifesto-style** opening.

### Challenge 3: "How do I make engineering feel warm and spiritual content feel rigorous?"
**Look for:** Sites that intentionally break the expected aesthetic of their industry. An engineer's site that feels **warm and crafted** (not cold and corporate). A ministry site that feels **clean and modern** (not cheesy or dated). The tension is the brand.

### Challenge 4: "How do I honor Nigerian/Yoruba identity without becoming 'ethnic' themed?"
**Look for:** How premium African brands (Paystack, Flutterwave) handle cultural identity — it's in the **confidence and groundedness**, not in tribal patterns pasted over a template. Study the **tone** more than the visuals.

### Challenge 5: "How do I create depth and invite exploration?"
**Look for:** Sites that use **progressive disclosure** — revealing more as you scroll or click. Homepages that feel like the "surface of something larger." Study **storytelling scroll patterns** and **layered content architecture**.

## 6. Suggested Process

1. **Spend 30-60 min** browsing the comparable sites above — not to copy, but to absorb what works
2. **Search Pinterest/Dribbble** with 4-5 keyword combos from the lists above
3. **Save 20-30 screenshots** into a folder or Pinterest board
4. **Tag each** with 2-3 mood keywords from the table above
5. **Look for the 3-4 screenshots that feel most "Israel"** — the ones where you think *"yes, this is the energy"*
6. **Share those with me** and I'll translate the mood into actual design decisions

---

*The goal isn't to find a template to copy. It's to find the **emotional frequency** the site should broadcast — and then build something original that transmits on that frequency.*
